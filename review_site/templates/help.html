Help & Glossary

Plain-English definitions of the terms you'll see while reviewing matches. Every match needs your confirmation — the system never approves anything on its own.

The Basics

NPI
National Provider Identifier — a unique 10-digit number the U.S. government assigns to every healthcare provider. The goal of this tool is to find the correct NPI for each HHL provider.
NPPES
The federal registry (run by CMS) that lists every NPI and its provider's name, address, credential, and specialty. The "candidates" you review come from this registry.
HHL record
The provider or medical center as it appears in Help Hope Live's own data — the thing we're trying to match to an official NPPES record.
Candidate
A possible NPPES match the system found for an HHL record. Each record can have several candidates, ranked best-first. Your job is to confirm the right one (or none).

How Matches Are Scored

Match score
A 0–1 number summarizing how closely a candidate resembles the HHL record across all signals. Higher is closer.
Candidate strength
STRONG MODERATE WEAK — how much evidence backs this candidate, based on the match score alone.
Confidence
How strong the match evidence is — the label to act on. Every match still needs your confirmation, even HIGH:
HIGH  Strong evidence — name plus confirming signals. Usually quick to verify.MEDIUM  Partial evidence — verify the details before confirming.LOW  Weak or single-signal evidence — check carefully; often a reject.
Strength vs. confidence
A candidate can be strong but not high-confidence — e.g. two equally good candidates make the choice uncertain even though each looks strong. Strength describes the candidate; confidence describes the decision.

"Why This Matched" — Signals

Each ✓ signal is one field that agreed between the HHL record and the NPPES candidate. More signals = more confidence.

name
The provider/center name matched closely. name_good means a near match just below the strong-name bar.
city / zip
The practice city or ZIP code matched.
credential
The NPPES credential fits the HHL role (e.g. LCSW for a Social Worker, MD for a Physician).
taxonomy
The NPPES specialty code (taxonomy) fits the HHL role. Taxonomy is the official NUCC specialty classification, e.g. 1041C0700X = Clinical Social Worker.
phone
The phone number matched exactly — a strong confirmation.
unique
This candidate stood clearly ahead of the next-best one, with no close competitor.
anchor
The search was narrowed to a confirmed medical center's location, which makes a professional match more precise.

Badges & Flags

★ National match
Rare and special. The system searched the entire country and found exactly one provider with this name — registered in a different state than the HHL record. It appears when no good match exists in the expected state (e.g. the provider moved, works for a multi-state system, or lives near a state border). Always confirm the state makes sense for this person.
· exact name
The name matched perfectly, character-for-character.
national_name_only
A National match supported only by the name — the credential and specialty couldn't be confirmed, so it's capped at MEDIUM. Verify the role before approving.
city_conflict
The candidate's city disagrees with the HHL record. May still be right (people move), but check.
margin_too_close
Two candidates scored almost the same, so picking one over the other is uncertain. Compare them carefully.
sibling_location
An organization has more than one valid location/NPI. Both may be correct — record each NPI that applies.
Placeholder entry
The HHL record is a generic placeholder (e.g. "Primary Physician") with no real name to match. Request more information from the patient.

Your Review Actions

Confirm / Approve
You agree this candidate is the correct match. Click once to arm (turns amber and asks "Confirm?"), click again to save — this prevents accidental approvals.
Flag
Set the record aside for a second look or a question — neither approved nor rejected.
No Match
None of the candidates is correct. Use this when the right provider simply isn't among the options.
Mark Complete
For centers with multiple valid NPIs — finish the record after approving every location that applies.
Compare
Opens a side-by-side view of the HHL record and a candidate so you can check the fields line up.
Profile
Opens the full NPPES record for a candidate, including all addresses and specialties.
